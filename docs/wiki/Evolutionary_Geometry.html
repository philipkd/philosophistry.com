Evolutionary Geometry
  • Abstract: random materials bouncing around with no form (Ocean, 4.5 b.y.a)
  • Single point: materials clump into one-dimensional dots or cells, still floating (Prokaryotes, 3.5 b.y.a.)
  • Linear: dots elongate into lines to aid in digestion (Bilateria, 570 m.y.a.)
  • Rectangular: lines widen along a plane to aid in mobility, as in fish
  • Tablet: upright for social displays
  • Network: long-distance communicators, nodes
